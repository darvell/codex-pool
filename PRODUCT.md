# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Friends of PP who are technically comfortable but impatient. They understand terminals, API keys, models, and developer tools, but expect the pool to turn shared infrastructure into a fast copy-paste setup rather than an operations exercise.

## Product Purpose

Codex Pool gives a trusted private group one place to access pooled AI providers, configure their preferred clients and SDKs, and understand account health, capacity, usage, and economic value. Success means a friend can enter, choose a setup path, get working quickly, and return later to see an honest account of what the pool can currently provide.

## Brand Personality

Private, arcane, and assured. The interface should feel like entry into a small, technically capable club rather than enrollment in a public SaaS product. Its atmosphere should be distinctive and slightly ceremonial without becoming ironic, theatrical, or difficult to operate.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic AI dashboard: interchangeable rounded cards, neon gradients, decorative glass, oversized hero metrics, excessive pills, or a template-like SaaS shell. Do not flatten the existing black, gold, heraldic, terminal-adjacent identity into conventional admin software. Avoid visual novelty that obscures setup instructions or makes live pool data feel ornamental rather than trustworthy.

## Design Principles

1. Make access feel earned, then make operation effortless.
2. Preserve the private-club identity while refining hierarchy, readability, and restraint.
3. Put the shortest working setup path first; reveal raw configuration for users who want to inspect it.
4. Show pool limits, availability, and economics honestly, including missing or temporary upstream data.
5. Use atmosphere as identity, not as chrome around every object.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Visual character takes priority over strict conformance where the two genuinely conflict, but core workflows must remain usable with keyboard navigation, visible focus, clear labels, comfortable touch targets, and responsive layouts. Preserve reduced-motion behavior and avoid relying on color alone for status or meaning. Low-light contrast may remain atmospheric in decorative and tertiary text, while setup commands, controls, errors, and live usage data must stay readily legible.
